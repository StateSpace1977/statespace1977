**From 500m to 42.195km** 

**My Mumbai Marathon Journey** 

I was never into fitness. Running even 500 meters used to feel exhausting, and with flat feet running always felt uncomfortable. For a long time I never imagined that one day I would run a full marathon. But life sometimes changes direction when the right people appear at the right time. Piyush Bhatt was the person who pushed me to attempt my first serious run — a 21 km half marathon. 

That moment slowly changed my relationship with running. What started as a small challenge turned into a consistent journey. Over the next 1.5 years my distances gradually increased — 5 km runs became regular, 10 km runs became comfortable, and the dream of completing a full marathon slowly started to feel possible.

**Race Day – Planning and Patience** 

Running 42 kilometers is not just about physical strength. It requires careful planning. Before the race I prepared my gear, nutrition and hydration strategy. Bananas and dry fruits helped fuel my body before the run. Energy gels were planned every few kilometers, and electrolytes helped maintain hydration during the race. 

I divided the 42 km marathon into smaller sections so that the distance felt manageable. Instead of thinking about the entire race, I focused on the next few kilometers. This mental strategy helped me stay calm and steady throughout the run. 

The real challenge begins after the 30 km mark. This is the point where fatigue starts to appear and the body begins asking you to slow down. At that moment the marathon becomes more mental than physical. Every step forward becomes a small victory.

**More Than Just a Race** 

What surprised me the most during the marathon was the incredible support from people across the city. Strangers were cheering from the streets, volunteers were encouraging every runner, and children were offering water, fruits and smiles to tired athletes. It was a beautiful reminder of how supportive people can be. 

I also received amazing support from friends during the race. Ritik Chawre was present at several cheering points along the route. Seeing familiar faces during a long race gives you a sudden burst of energy and motivation. 

When I finally crossed the finish line and received my medal, it felt like much more than completing a race. It represented months of training, discipline, patience and belief. The marathon changed my perspective — it showed me that progress happens step by step, kilometer by kilometer.
